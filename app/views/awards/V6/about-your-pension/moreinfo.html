Claim your NHS Pension

Do you want to apply for trivial commutation?

Trivial commutation is when a small pension can be paid as a one off payment when you retire.

We can send you more details about this, if the value of all of your benefits added together (capital value) is between £6,000 and £30,000. If they are less than £6,000, we'll automatically send you the information you'll need.

The capital value is calculated by multiplying your annual pension income by 20 and adding any retirement lump sum.

When will I get the payment?

You get this when you retire, either:

  • on ill health at any age
  • on or after age 55 (or age 50, if you have a protected minimum pension age)
If your capital value is higher than £30,000

If the capital value of your NHS benefits and any other pension benefits with other providers is more than £30,000, it may still be possible to pay your NHS benefits as a one off payment, if that one off payment is less than £10,000.

If you don’t have this information available, you can return to your application summary and save your application for later.
