What is allocation?

Allocation is where you permanently give up part of your pension now to your spouse, civil partner or a dependant. If you die, the allocated person will receive this amount as a pension.

If you allocate to anyone other than a spouse or civil partner, they must be dependant on you at the time of your retirement and when you die.

If you allocate, it will provide an additional payment on top of any adult dependant's pension or children’s pension that may be payable.

To apply, you’ll have to pay for a medical. We can send you guidance on this.

How is this different to a death benefit nomination?

You can nominate someone to receive an adult dependant's pension and lump sum on death benefit if you die.

This does not affect the amount of pension you receive.
