Where to find out what benefit type you're getting

There are some benefits with similar names.
Check the payment letter you got from the Department for Work & Pensions (DWP).

Select 'No' if you get one of these benefits:

  • Contribution-based Job Seekers Allowance (JSA)
  • Contribution-based Employment Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Pension Credit and Savings Credit

If you're still unsure what benefit you get contact Jobcentre Plus

Employment and Support Allowance

There are 2 types of ESA, income related and contribution based.

The type of ESA you get is usually shown on page 3, under the heading ‘Income and Benefits’. It will say either:

  • 'your income-related entitlement is'

    • or

  • 'you are entitled to contribution based'

Job Seekers Allowance

There are 2 types of JSA, income based and contribution based.

The type of JSA you get is shown on page 3 or 4, under the heading ‘How your Jobseeker's Allowance was worked out’. It will say either:

  • 'We call this Income-based Jobseeker's Allowance, or
  • 'We call this contribution-based'Jobseeker's Allowance.
